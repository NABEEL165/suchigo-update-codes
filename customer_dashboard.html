Waste Handling Guidelines

Segregation Guidelines

Organic Waste

Food scraps, vegetable peels, coffee grounds, and garden waste should be placed in green bins. Keep them separate from other waste types.

Recyclable Waste

Paper, plastic, glass, and metal items should be clean and dry before placing in blue bins. Remove any food residue from containers.

Non-Recyclable Waste

Items that cannot be recycled, such as dirty plastics, ceramics, and sanitary waste, should go in grey bins.

Hazardous Waste

Electronic Waste

Batteries, electronic devices, and light bulbs should be disposed of at designated collection centers. Do not mix with regular waste.

Chemical Waste

Paints, solvents, pesticides, and cleaning chemicals should be kept separate and disposed of according to local regulations.

Collection Best Practices

Timing

Place your bins at the curb by 7:00 AM on your scheduled collection day. Ensure they are easily accessible to collection crews.

Preparation

Keep bins clean and ensure lids are closed. Do not overfill bins as this can cause spillage during collection.
